## What each side had

**Parent 1** had all fields in both `ActivitiComponent` and `ActivitiEndpoint` declared as `private`. The method `createEndpoint` used parameter name `stringObjectMap`. No `HistoryService` field existed.

**Parent 2** had promoted all fields from `private` to `protected` in both classes (enabling subclassing), added a new `HistoryService historyService` field, and renamed the parameter `stringObjectMap` → `parameters` in `createEndpoint`.

## Interpretation

P2 performed two distinct refactorings that conflicted with P1:

### 1. Replace_Attribute — `private` → `protected` visibility on all fields

All fields in `ActivitiComponent` (`identityService`, `runtimeService`, `copyVariablesToProperties`, `copyVariablesToBodyAsMap`, `copyCamelBodyToBody`) and `ActivitiEndpoint` (same fields plus `activitiConsumer`, `copyVariablesFromProperties`, `copyVariablesFromHeader`) were promoted from `private` to `protected`. The `--` lines remove `private` declarations and the `++` lines introduce `protected` ones across both classes — a systematic **Replace_Attribute** (visibility modifier change) applied to 9 fields over 2 files.

### 2. Rename_Parameter — `stringObjectMap` → `parameters`

The parameter of `createEndpoint` was renamed from the opaque `stringObjectMap` to the clearer `parameters`. This is a direct **Rename_Parameter** with `--`/`++` evidence at the method declaration.

Additionally, the new `HistoryService historyService` field (line `++`) is introduced as part of the merge resolving the conflict — P2 added this field alongside the visibility changes, and the merge had to incorporate it in the correct position within the field list.
