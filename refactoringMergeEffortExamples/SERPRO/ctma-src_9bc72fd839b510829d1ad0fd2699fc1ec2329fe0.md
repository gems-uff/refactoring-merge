## Interpretation

Two overlapping refactorings collide here:

1. **Merge_Parameter** (evidenced in P1): P1 replaced six independent `int count*` parameters of `includedCustomsNoteUnique` with a single `Map<String, Integer> mapCount`, and likewise for `includedCustomsNoteInConhecimentoNaoIATA`. The P2 branch still used the old multi-parameter signatures.

2. **Remove_Parameter / parameter cleanup** (P1 also): P1 removed the `Recebimento recebimento` leading parameter from both methods; P2 still had it.

The conflict arose because the two parents had incompatible signatures for the same private methods. The merge had to produce the final signatures (`++` lines) that reconcile both changes: keeping P1's `Map<String, Integer> mapCount` consolidation while dropping P2's `Recebimento` parameter and individual counters.

The `++` lines on the call site (`includedCustomsNoteInConhecimentoNaoIATA(conhecimento, mapCount, ...)`) and on both method declarations (`includedCustomsNoteUnique(Conhecimento conhecimento, Map<String, Integer> mapCount, MultiBusinessException mbe)`) are direct merge effort caused by the signature conflict. The refactoring (Merge_Parameter) was unambiguously introduced in Parent 1, not by the merge itself, as evidenced by the `" -"` lines showing P2 still carrying the old individual-counter signatures.
