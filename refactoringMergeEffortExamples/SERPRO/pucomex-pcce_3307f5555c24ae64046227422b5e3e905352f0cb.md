1. **Rename_Method**
   - `receberNotificacaoCargaEntregue` → `registrarEntregaCarga`
   - `receberNotificacaoCancelamentoEntregaCarga` → `registrarCancelamentoEntregaCarga`
   The `++` call to `duimpSolicitacaoContext.registrarEntregaCarga(...)` / `registrarCancelamentoEntregaCarga(...)` and the new method headers confirm the rename pair.

2. **Split_Parameter**
   - The single DTO parameter `NotificacaoCargaEntregueDTO notificacaoCargaEntregue` was divided into its three semantically-related fields: `String nrDeclaracao`, `String identificadorCarga`, `String cpfResponsavel`. The same holds for `NotificacaoCancelamentoCargaEntregueDTO` → the same three Strings.
   - This satisfies the strict definition (one existing parameter divided into two or more semantically related parts, not the addition of an independent parameter). The one-to-one mapping is proven directly by the merge-effort lines: `notificacaoCargaEntregue.getNumDuimp()` → `nrDeclaracao`, `notificacaoCargaEntregue.getNumIdentificacaoCarga()` → `identificadorCarga`, and `notificacaoCargaEntregue.getCpfResponsavelEntrega()` → `cpfResponsavel`. The trailing DTO object that Parent 2 passed as a final `MessageFormat` argument (`{4}` / `{2}` / `{3}`) is dropped in the `++` lines precisely because the unified DTO no longer exists in the signature.

**Why the `++` lines confirm it.** Each `++` line is a Parent‑2 statement re‑expressed in terms of Parent‑1's new parameters.
