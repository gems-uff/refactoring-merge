## What each side had

**Parent 1 (P1):**
- All authorization admin service constructors accepted `RealmAuth auth` as the permission-checking parameter.
- Auth checks used `this.auth.requireManage()`, `this.auth.requireView()`, etc.
- `findOrCreateResourceServer(ClientModel)` in `MgmtPermissions` did a direct store lookup.
- `RolePermissions.resourceServer(RoleModel)` and `ClientPermissions.resourceServer(ClientModel)` called the store directly.

**Parent 2 (P2):**
- Replaced `RealmAuth` with `AdminPermissionEvaluator` across all authorization admin service constructors and field declarations.
- Updated all auth-check call sites to use the new `auth.realm().requireManageAuthorization()` / `auth.realm().requireViewAuthorization()` style.
- Centralized resource-server lookup in `MgmtPermissions.resourceServer(ClientModel)` redirecting to `realmResourceServer()`.
- Updated `RolePermissions` and `ClientPermissions` to delegate to `root.resourceServer(client)`.

## Interpretation

**Replace_Attribute / Change_Parameter_Type** (systematic across nine authorization admin service classes): P2 replaced the type of the `auth` field from `RealmAuth` to `AdminPermissionEvaluator` in all affected classes, and correspondingly changed all constructor parameters. The `--` lines on `RealmAuth auth` and the `++` lines on `AdminPermissionEvaluator auth` in constructor signatures and field declarations across `AuthorizationService`, `PolicyService`, `PolicyResourceService`, `PermissionService`, `PolicyTypeService`, `PolicyTypeResourceService`, `ResourceServerService`, `ResourceSetService`, and `ScopeService` are all merge-effort lines caused by the conflict between P1 (unchanged `RealmAuth` call sites) and P2's type replacement.

**Move_Method** (`findOrCreateResourceServer` logic ŌåÆ centralized in `MgmtPermissions`): P2 moved the resource-server lookup out of individual classes (`RolePermissions`, `ClientPermissions`) and into `MgmtPermissions.resourceServer(ClientModel)`. The `--` lines removing direct `authz.getStoreFactory().getResourceServerStore().findByClient(...)` calls and the `++` lines delegating to `root.resourceServer(client)` are merge-effort propagating this Move_Method.

Both cases are strongly supported by consistent `++`/`--` patterns across multiple files in the combined diff.
