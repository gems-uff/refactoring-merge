## What each side had

**Parent 1 (P1)** had the original HA-state machine where `HAState` methods (`setState`, `enterState`, `exitState`, `checkOperation`, `setStateInternal`) all accepted `NameNode nn` as a direct parameter, and `FSNamesystem` had methods named `activateSecretManager()` and `activate(conf)`. `NameNode` called `namesystem.activate(conf)` directly.

**Parent 2 (P2)** introduced a new `HAContext` interface to decouple the state machine from `NameNode` concretely, and renamed `FSNamesystem.activate()` to `startCommonServices()` (and `activateSecretManager()` to `startSecrectManager()`). All `HAState` methods were updated to accept `HAContext context` instead of `NameNode nn`.

## Interpretation

This case exhibits two tightly coupled refactorings introduced in P2 before the merge:

1. **Rename_Method** (`activate` → `startCommonServices` in `FSNamesystem`; `activateSecretManager` → `startSecrectManager`): The `--` lines show `void activate(Configuration conf)` and `void activateSecretManager()` being removed, while the `++` lines introduce `void startCommonServices(Configuration conf)` and `void startSecrectManager()`. The call site in `NameNode.initialize()` is correspondingly updated from `activate(conf)` (with `--`) to `startCommonServices(conf)` (with `++`). This is a clear **Rename_Method** that the merge had to propagate.

2. **Change_Parameter_Type** (`NameNode nn` → `HAContext context` in `HAState` and subclasses): P2 introduced an `HAContext` interface so that `HAState` methods no longer depend directly on the concrete `NameNode` class. The `--` lines throughout `HAState.java`, `ActiveState.java`, and `StandbyState.java` show `NameNode nn` as the parameter type, while the `++` lines replace every occurrence with `HAContext context`. All call sites in `NameNode` that previously passed `this` (e.g., `state.setState(this, ...)`) are updated to pass `haContext`. This is a textbook **Change_Parameter_Type** refactoring, confirmed across five files with consistent `++`/`--` patterns throughout.

The merge effort (`++`/`--` lines) is the work required to reconcile both changes simultaneously — propagating the renaming and the parameter-type change across all affected classes in a single merge commit.
